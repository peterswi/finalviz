Are We Feeding Our People?

A look at Food Insecurity in the United States

Learn more about the background to this project Here.

Food Insecurity (FI): describes a household's inability to provide enough food for every person to live an active, healthy life; it is one way we can measure and assess the risk of hunger (Source)

Food insecurity is a major issue in the United States. When members of a household do not know where their next meal will come from, they are less likely to be able to focus things such as school and work, which can had adverse effects on career opportunities, finances, confidence, and more. Feeding America is an organization focused on meeting the needs of families across the country by drawing awareness to issues such as hunger, poverty, and food insecurity, and taking action to put an end to such suffering.

We obtained data from Feeding America on the state of food insecurity in the United States from 2009-2018. This data provided food insecurity rates by state, the number of food insecure individuals, childhood poverty and food insecurity, among other elements. We chose to take an exploratory approach, evaluating the state of food insecurity in the US and investigating how it has changed over time by state.

We hope to emphasize the magnitude of this issue and draw attention to what states are suffering most.

One may assume the levels of child poverty would correlate with child food insecurity.
